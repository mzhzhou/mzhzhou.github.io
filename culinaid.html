Beyond purely text-based AI interactions
As Generative AI gains more footing in our daily interactions, it is increasingly important to be designing these interactions to accommodate wider populations and use cases. Current chat interaction and information gathering tools mostly rely on text to communicate. However, a purely text-based interaction can be ineffective and have a high cognitive load for many users.
One use case scenario of this is in recipe generation - a creative, visual, knowledge-based and sensory-inducing process.
Source: Claude.ai, Gemini, ChatGPT
Dual Coding Theory
Dual coding theory, created by Allan Paivio, states that the mind processes information along two different subsystems: verbal and imagery. This theory highlights the importance of combining dual modes to enhance information retention as it provides individuals with multiple processing approaches and memory retrieval channels.
As current interaction methods with GenAI only utilize the verbal channel, we examine whether the usage of visuals and interactivity can further facilitate human learning from GenAI tools.

Do supporting visual diagrams help with information retention?
One of the core features we wanted to test for this study was whether supporting visual graphics would help users with information retention and engagement. These might come in a variety of formats and look like knowledge graphs, mind maps, images, highlighted text, or color blocks, just to name a few.
After an exploration of a number of different styles, we narrowed down the study to the integration of images and knowledge graphs as we felt that these were the most suited formats that are common to recipe writing.
Research Design
Our study aimed to explore whether Dual Coding Theory has a place in chatbot interactions. In order to evaluate this, we needed to establish the metrics of ‘success’ - whether users were left with useful information and the inspiration to cook. Each participant was shown a number of chatbot interaction options and asked to self-report their takeaways post-study. Participants had varying levels of familiarity with GenAI tools and some familiarity with cooking.
Note: Before the experiment, each participant was given a consent form that stated the purpose and expectations of the experiment and allowed participants to opt-in.
Prototype Design
We created the tool using Node, Javascript and HTML/CSS built atop GPT 3.5 Turbo and Dall-E 3.
With a minimal interface design, each of the experiment options are built into the chatbot so that only the necessary components could be measured. Key features of the tool are:
An ability to use conversational text prompts to generate both visual and text-based AI responses Ability to input generated text into an image generator Ability to collaborate and provide suggestions Ability to visually format responses within chatbot-based environments
Results indicate a preference for visual elements
One of the key learnings from the study is that no participant preferred the text-only option when given the experiment. All participants felt inclined towards an option with a dual visual-textual interaction. This showcases user preference towards GenAI outputs that have a visual component, which could be a valuable insight to increase user adoption of the technology itself.
However, we also found that people have different preferences for how information is visualized, and we hypothesize that these preferences could vary even more in different use cases, which needs further exploration.
Introducing Culinaid
Culinaid is a recipe generation tool that allows home cook users to generate recipes and help them through the ideation process as they decided what they want to cook. It utilises generated visual aids to help users quickly process and recall information.
